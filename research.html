Swarm Robotic Manipulation

Decentralized Control for Construction-Oriented Tasks | Thesis 2024

Designed and integrated a five-bar parallel SCARA manipulator for the QUPA differential robot, enabling cooperative construction tasks through decentralized swarm coordination. The system combines computer vision for object detection with a modular electronic architecture — fabricated entirely via additive manufacturing.

96% Grasping success rate
±4 mm Positioning accuracy
58.5% Task time reduction (3 robots vs 1)
>90% Aggregation efficiency
  • Stack: ROS (Noetic), Python, C++, OpenCV.
  • Simulation: ARGoS (swarm) & Gazebo (embodiment).
  • Autonomy: 106 min under max load.
  • Status: Simulation-validated; physical deployment constrained by hardware assembly timeline — ongoing.

Autonomous Navigation

Intership at Robotics and Intelligent Systems Engineer Lab | 2024 (RISE) - Remote

Engineered a comprehensive simulation pipeline using ROS 2 and Gazebo to validate navigation in unstructured environments with dynamic human actors. The system integrates SLAM and AMCL for localization, while benchmarking local controllers like MPC and DWA to optimize dynamic obstacle avoidance and path planning.

  • Stack: ROS (Noetic), ROS2 (Humble), Python, C++.
  • Simulation: Gazebo Classic and Fortress.
  • Controllers: DWA, MPC, MPPI, RL variants
