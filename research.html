Swarm Robotic Manipulation

Decentralized Control for Construction-Oriented Tasks | Thesis 2024

This thesis focused on designing a manipulation module for a local designed differential robot, enabling them to pick up objects from the environment. Leveraging this mechanism, we developed a decentralized swarm strategy for gathering and transporting chips, achieving faster grouping efficiency without central control.

  • Stack: ROS (Noetic), Python, C++.
  • Simulation: ARGoS & Gazebo.
  • Result: Validated with 12 agents.

Autonomous Navigation

Intership at Robotics and Intelligent Systems Engineer Lab | 2024 (RISE) - Remote

Engineered a comprehensive simulation pipeline using ROS 2 and Gazebo to validate navigation in unstructured environments with dynamic human actors. The system integrates SLAM and AMCL for localization, while benchmarking local controllers like MPC and DWA to optimize dynamic obstacle avoidance and path planning.

  • Stack: ROS (Noetic), ROS2 (Humble), Python, C++.
  • Simulation: Gazebo Classic and Fortress.
  • Controllers: DWA, MPC, MPPI, RL variants

Industrial Prosthetic Prototype | Bio-Mechatronics

  • CAD/CAM: Designed in Fusion 360 optimized for 3D printing.
  • Materials: PLA+ and PETG for structural integrity.
