About Me

I am a Mechatronics Engineer and researcher at the Collective Robotics and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (CoRAL). My work focuses on autonomous robots and collective behaviors, specifically exploring bio-inspired swarms to solve complex coordination challenges

While my current research is technical and decentralized, I am driven by the long-term vision of a world where robots and humans coexist seamlessly. I combine my background in international research collaborations with extensive experience in industrial automation and electrical design, a skill set I developed to meet the technical demands of the industrial landscape in my home country. I am also a passionate educator, currently serving as a Lab Technician at ESPOL, where I lead practice sessions in mobile and articulated robotics.

Research and International Collaboration

  • Bio-Inspired Swarm Construction: Collaborate under the supervision of Ph.D. Christian Tutiven Head of Collaborative Robotics and Artificial Intelligence Lab (CoRAL) and Ph.D. David Garzon Ramos University, professor at the University of College Dublin (UCD) in the design of a modular robotic actuator for a swarm platform and decentralized behaviors for collective manipulation.
  • Autonomous Navigation Pipelines: Worked with Ph.D. Nabih Pico in collaboration with the Robotics and Intelligent Systems Engineering Laboratory (RISE Lab) at Sungkyunkwan University (SKKU), South Korea, to develop and benchmark Nav2-based architectures and perception pipelines.
  • Multi-Fidelity Simulation: Deep knowledge in building simulation-to-control infrastructures using ARGoS for large-scale swarm experiments and ROS/Gazebo for physically grounded robot embodiment.
  • Core Competencies: Through these international projects, I have mastered rapid prototyping (3D printing), complex system simulation, and cross-border teamwork.
