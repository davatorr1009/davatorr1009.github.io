About Me

I am a Mechatronics Engineer and researcher at the Collective Robotics and Artificial Intelligence Laboratory (CoRAL). My work focuses on autonomous robots and collective behaviors, specifically exploring bio-inspired swarms to solve complex coordination challenges.


While my current research is technical and decentralized, I am driven by the long-term vision of a world where robots and humans coexist seamlessly. I combine my background in international research collaborations with extensive experience in industrial automation and electrical design, a skill set I developed to meet the technical demands of the industrial landscape in my home country. I am also a passionate educator, currently serving as a Lab Technician at ESPOL, where I lead practice sessions in mobile and articulated robotics.

Research and International Collaboration

Bio-Inspired Swarm

Collaborated under the supervision of Ph.D. Christian Tutiven (CoRAL Lab) and Ph.D. David Garzon Ramos (UCD) in the design of a modular robotic actuator and decentralized behaviors for collective manipulation.

Navigation Pipelines

Worked with Ph.D. Nabih Pico at SKKU (South Korea) within the RISE Lab to develop and benchmark Nav2-based architectures and perception pipelines using LiDAR and depth cameras.

Multi-Fidelity Sim

Deep knowledge in building simulation-to-control infrastructures using ARGoS for large-scale swarm experiments and ROS/Gazebo for physically grounded robot embodiment.

Core Competencies

Through these international projects, I have mastered rapid prototyping (3D printing), complex system simulation, and cross-border teamwork.
